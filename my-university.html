Rutgers University – Newark

Below is some information and historical background about Rutgers Newark, where I am currently taking classes in a second-degree (undergraduate) program. Note that additional information can be found on Wikipedia.

  • Rutgers University–Newark is one of the three regional campuses of Rutgers University, a public land-grant research university in New Jersey. It is located in Newark’s University Heights on a 38-acre urban campus.
  • Rutgers was originally founded in 1766 in New Brunswick, making it the eighth oldest college in the United States. The Newark campus was formed in 1946 when the former University of Newark was merged into Rutgers, following its designation as the state university in 1945.
  • Today, Rutgers–Newark enrolls over 12,000 students across undergraduate and graduate programs. It is classified as an R2: Doctoral University – High Research Activity and hosts seven degree-granting schools, including the School of Public Affairs and Administration, Rutgers Business School, and Rutgers Law School.
  • The campus is home to several notable research centers, including the Institute of Jazz Studies. Rutgers–Newark is also known for its academic partnerships, such as cross-registration with the neighboring New Jersey Institute of Technology (NJIT).
  • According to U.S. News & World Report, Rutgers–Newark is recognized as the most diverse national university in the United States.
